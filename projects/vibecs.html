VibeCS

LIVE · REVENUE

Zero-config AI customer support. Paste your URL → crawler builds the knowledge base → embed one script tag → live in under three minutes. It has paying customers.

cs-vibe.com

problem

Intercom and Zendesk assume a support team and a week of setup. Indie builders have neither – so their sites either have no support, or a generic bot that cheerfully hallucinates about a product it never read. The people who most need automated support are exactly the people who will never configure it. The setup step isn't a hurdle; it's why the category doesn't serve them.

built

  • Setup deleted, not simplified. Paste a URL; the crawler reads your landing, docs, FAQ, and pricing; one async script tag later the agent is live. The knowledge base is your website, re-crawled weekly.
  • Closed-loop, so it can't hallucinate. Answers come only from crawled content – outside the loop, it says "I don't know." That's a trust architecture, not a limitation.
  • Personas over prompt engineering. "The Buddy" or "The Engineer." Pick a personality; skip the system prompt.
  • The widget is the funnel. Every embed carries "Powered by VibeCS." Footprint = distribution.
  • It built its own sales team. Nightly pipeline: six directory scrapers, a detector that skips anyone who already has chat, three-layer founder-email discovery, self-healing bounce handling. 120 products in, 23 personalized emails out, zero failures.

status

Live, with real revenue – paying customers acquired by the pipeline it runs on itself. Dogfooded across the portfolio: VibeCaddy's site runs the widget; BlockRate's did too. Pricing: free to test · $8/mo · $50 lifetime.

The customers worth winning are builders and operators paying for leverage – not consumers hunting discounts. BlockRate taught it; VibeCS confirmed it.
