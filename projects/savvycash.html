SavvyCash Beta

Your money, always working. An iOS app that watches your accounts, learns your spending rhythm, and proposes sweeps into high-yield savings — but never moves a dollar without you.

The Problem

Most people keep $3,000–$10,000 in checking earning 0.01% while high-yield savings pays 4–5%. That's hundreds of dollars a year, silently forfeited — not out of ignorance, but friction: sweeping money by hand means predicting your own bills, and nobody wants that recurring homework. The pain is real but latent; you don't feel it until someone shows you the number.

What I Built

SavvyCash does the homework. It links accounts through Plaid, learns your actual spending rhythm, and proposes the sweep — sized and timed so the money is back before your bills land.

  • Patterns, not rules No manual budgeting rules to configure. The engine has detected 21+ recurring spending patterns and predicts 12+ upcoming bills — including credit-card due dates pulled from Plaid's Liabilities API — from transaction history alone.
  • Propose, never execute Every sweep is a proposal the user approves. That's a trust decision first — an app that silently moves your money has to earn something no beta can — and it deliberately sidesteps ACH compliance complexity for v1.
  • Closed loop Predictions come only from your own Plaid data — no external signals, no black-box scores. The same closed-loop conviction as VibeCS, applied to money.
Stack
iOS
Swift 5.9+, SwiftUI with @Observable (iOS 17+)
Banking
Plaid — Transactions and Liabilities APIs, production linking live
Backend
Supabase — Postgres and Edge Functions (Deno/TypeScript)
Status & Outcome

In beta, unreleased — and honestly mid-build. Plaid production linking, transaction sync, pattern detection, and bill prediction all work today. The sweep-proposal engine is still being tuned, executed transfers aren't wired yet, and there's an open product question I haven't answered: is this an autopilot, or a co-pilot whose entire value is the intelligence layer?

It's also the portfolio's deliberate stretch: a consumer fintech app from someone whose winning pattern is builder tools — with the App Store, my home turf, as the distribution edge that might make the exception work.
