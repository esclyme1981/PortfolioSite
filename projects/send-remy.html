Send Remy Live

An agent-first iOS dating app. Your Remy talks to their Remy — so you stop wasting nights on people you were never going to like.

sendremy.com App Store
The Problem

Dating apps run on weeks of low-signal texting. Two tired people trade "hey"s and logistics until one of them gives up, and the ones who persist often discover on date one what a single honest conversation would have surfaced in minutes. For the app-fatigued 28-to-42 crowd — urban, time-poor, actually looking for a relationship — the blank-slate first chat is the single most expensive step in the funnel.

What I Built

Send Remy gives each person an agent. A conversational onboarding interview builds your persona; from then on, when you like someone in the daily deck, your agent and theirs hold an async conversation of 12–20 turns — covering the compatibility ground humans take weeks to reach. You get back a debrief: a compatibility score, highlight moments, and honest tension points. Only when both sides opt in does a human chat open — seeded with tappable first messages drawn from what the agents already learned. No blank slates, no "hey."

  • Date-four energy on date one The goal isn't to "move past looks" — it's to arrive at the first date with the getting-to-know-you already done. The product works with attraction, not against it.
  • The debrief is the product Compatibility score, transcript highlights, and tension points turn a swipe into an informed decision. Even a non-match teaches you something about what you want.
  • Monetization maps to engagement Freemium: three agent sends a day, pay for more. Rate limits are framed in-world — "your agents are busy" — not as a paywall.

Status & Outcome

Live on the App Store, launched in Austin and Los Angeles. Designed, built, and shipped solo — product spec through Swift code through App Store review. The cold-start problem was de-risked before launch by testing agent conversations against synthetic personas, which is also how the debrief format was tuned.
