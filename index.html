ERIC(1) – General Commands Manual

NAME

eric – product manager who answers questions with working product

SYNOPSIS

eric [--prototype] [--ship-fast] [--mentor] [--kill-when-wrong]

DESCRIPTION

Documents describe problems. Product answers them. Everything in this directory was built end to end on modern agent tooling, because building the real thing quickly is the fastest way to find out what's true – about the user, the market, and the idea. It's the same job during the day: at SimplePractice I lead mobile and rapid prototyping across all of Product, where most of the work is getting real product in front of real people in days – and mentoring PMs and teams to do the same: reach for a build, not another meeting.

Low-key most valuable flag is --kill-when-wrong. BlockRate wasn't precious technology – it was a working marketplace with real revenue that was wrong underneath: wrong customer, bad sector. Recognizing "not right" in weeks instead of years, and acting on it, is (almost) as important as validating product-market-fit.

HISTORY

Fifteen years in product – three as a developer, five as a founder, more than a decade leading teams. The specifics are in career.log, below.

BUGS

Occasionally does stand-up. Opened for a couple of real comics – a humbling way to learn to read a room.
