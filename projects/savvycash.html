SavvyCash

BETA

"Your money, always working." An iOS app that watches your accounts through Plaid, learns your spending rhythm, and sweeps idle cash into high-yield savings automatically – sized and timed so the money is back before your bills land. In beta, every sweep required a user's approval.

problem

Most people park thousands in checking at 0.01% while high-yield savings pays 4–5% – hundreds of dollars a year, silently forfeited. Not ignorance; friction. Sweeping by hand means predicting your own bills, and nobody wants recurring homework. The pain is real but latent – you don't feel it until someone shows you the number.

built

  • Patterns, not rules. No budgeting config. The engine detects recurring spending patterns and predicts bills – including credit-card due dates from Plaid's Liabilities API – from transaction history alone.
  • Automated sweeps, beta-gated. The engine sizes, times, and executes transfers on its own. During beta, every sweep required explicit user approval – an app that moves your money has to earn that trust before it runs unattended.
  • Closed loop. Predictions come only from your own Plaid data – no external signals, no black-box scores. Same conviction as VibeCS, applied to money.

stack

ios
Swift 5.9+ · SwiftUI · @Observable (iOS 17+)
banking
Plaid – Transactions + Liabilities, production linking live
backend
Supabase – Postgres, Edge Functions (Deno/TS)

status

Beta, unreleased. Linking, sync, pattern detection, bill prediction, and automated sweeps all work; beta policy kept a human approval on every transfer while the engine earned trust. Still tuning before a wider release.

It's also the portfolio's deliberate stretch – consumer fintech from someone whose winning pattern is builder tools, with the App Store (my home turf) as the distribution edge that might make the exception work.
