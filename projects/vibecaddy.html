VibeCaddy Live

Your AI tools forget everything. VibeCaddy doesn't — it injects your project's stack, decisions, and conventions into every prompt, across every tool.

vibecaddy.ai
The Problem

Every AI session starts from zero. Builders working across ChatGPT, Claude, Lovable, and Bolt re-type the same paragraph dozens of times a day — the stack, the conventions, the decisions already made — and when they don't, they get generic answers that ignore all of it. The tools have no memory of your project, and the cost lands on you, one blank prompt at a time.

What I Built

VibeCaddy is a Chrome extension that intercepts your prompt before it's sent and prepends the relevant project context — pulled from VibeOS, the persistent memory layer underneath it. A vague five-word prompt leaves your keyboard as a fully contextualized instruction. It works across six-plus platforms: ChatGPT, Claude, Lovable, and Bolt fully supported; Base44 and Emergent in beta.

  • A memory layer, not a snippet manager Context is retrieved per-prompt by relevance, with guards against cross-project pollution — your dating-app facts never leak into your fintech prompts.
  • A production eval agent tunes it nightly Every night an eval agent scores the day's optimized prompts, adjusts retrieval similarity thresholds, and marks decayed facts stale. The product literally improves while I sleep — a self-tuning loop most teams never ship.
  • Priced like a tool, not a platform Free tier of five prompts a day; $8/month unlimited; $120 lifetime. The same pricing spine as VibeCS — indie tools for indie builders.

Status & Outcome

Live in the Chrome Web Store and used daily — including on every product in this portfolio, which makes it the most dogfooded thing I've shipped.

The honest product-market read: the resonance is real — nobody enjoys re-explaining their project — but distribution is throttled by Chrome's trust warnings on new extensions. That's a distribution problem, not a product problem, and knowing which one you have is the PM skill this project keeps exercising.
