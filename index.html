ERIC(1) — General Commands Manual

NAME

eric — product manager who ships his own specs

SYNOPSIS

eric [--spec] [--build] [--ship] [--kill-when-wrong]

DESCRIPTION

Most PMs hand off. I don't. Every product in this directory — iOS apps, a Chrome extension, SaaS — was spec'd, built, and shipped by the same pair of hands. The through-line is one opinion held strongly: software should show up already knowing the context. Agents that do the getting-to-know-you before a first date. A support bot whose knowledge base is your own website. Prompts that arrive pre-loaded with your stack. No blank slates, anywhere.

The flag I'm proudest of is --kill-when-wrong: BlockRate was the best engineering in this list and the worst business. It's dead because I read the data and pulled the plug. That's a PM skill you can't fake in a slide deck.

HISTORY

Group Product Manager at SimplePractice — leading both mobile and rapid prototyping across all of Product. Founded Timebomb — a 15-person mobile social startup, 5 years. Shipped a #1 paid App Store app internationally.
